# How AI Works — Research Report

## Overview
Artificial intelligence (AI) refers to the development of computer systems that can perform tasks that typically require human intelligence, such as learning, problem-solving, and decision-making. AI systems use a combination of algorithms, data, and hardware to process and analyze information, allowing them to make predictions, classify objects, and make decisions. The core of AI lies in its ability to learn from data and improve its performance over time.

## AI Architecture
The architecture of AI systems typically consists of three main components:

1. **Hardware**: This includes the physical components of the system, such as the central processing unit (CPU), memory, and storage.
2. **Software**: This includes the algorithms and programs that run on the hardware, such as machine learning frameworks and natural language processing tools.
3. **Data**: This includes the input data that the AI system uses to learn and make decisions, such as images, text, and sensor readings.

## Recent Developments
Recent advancements in AI have led to significant improvements in areas such as:

1. **Deep learning**: Techniques such as convolutional neural networks (CNNs) and recurrent neural networks (RNNs) have enabled AI systems to learn complex patterns in data.
2. **Natural language processing**: AI systems can now understand and generate human language, enabling applications such as chatbots and language translation.
3. **Computer vision**: AI systems can now recognize and classify objects in images and videos, enabling applications such as self-driving cars and surveillance systems.

## Key Takeaways
1. AI systems use a combination of algorithms, data, and hardware to process and analyze information.
2. The architecture of AI systems consists of hardware, software, and data components.
3. Recent advancements in AI have led to significant improvements in areas such as deep learning, natural language processing, and computer vision.
